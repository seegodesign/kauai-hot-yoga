Whether you're just unrolling your first yoga mat or you've been practicing for years, there's always room to grow. One of the most powerful ways to deepen your practice isn't necessarily by learning advanced poses—it's by mastering the fundamentals of frequency, accuracy, and precision.

These three concepts, often used in science and athletics, can unlock new awareness and consistency in your practice. They can also help to fine tune your body and mind! Here's how they apply to yoga:

## 1. Frequency: Show Up for Yourself

Frequency is how often you practice. It's the foundation of growth. The more consistently you practice, the more your body and mind adapt. Muscle memory improves, flexibility builds, and breath becomes more intuitive.

**How to build frequency:**

- Start small: Aim for just one, two, or three classes a week.
- Make it routine: Tie your yoga practice to something you already do—like waking up or winding down.
- Be flexible (pun intended): If you can't make your scheduled class, doing even 10 minutes of breathwork, meditation, or flow at home counts!

Think of frequency like watering a plant—little by little, it makes everything blossom.

## 2. Accuracy: Align with Intention

Accuracy in yoga means doing each pose correctly—with proper alignment and engagement. It's about understanding what a pose is asking of your body and meeting it where you are.

**How to improve accuracy:**

- Use props when appropriate: Blocks or straps can help you find alignment safely. Bolsters in a non-heated class can support your alignment as well!
- Get guidance: Follow skilled teachers or attend workshops to fine-tune your form. Ask teachers questions to better understand the form!
- Check in: Use the mirror, record your practice, or ask for feedback to notice patterns or misalignments.

Accurate movement helps prevent injury and allows your body to fully benefit from each pose.

## 3. Precision: Repeat with Consistency

Precision is about doing the right thing the same way over and over. It's the difference between hitting a perfect Warrior II once, and being able to land it every time with control and presence.

**How to build precision:**

- Slow down: Moving mindfully reveals where you're rushing or losing form.
- Revisit the basics: Poses like Downward Dog or Tree Pose teach balance and strength in every repetition.
- Use breath as your metronome: Let your breath guide your pace and keep you grounded.

Precision brings depth. It's what transforms a pose into a moving meditation.

## It's a Practice, Not a Performance

By weaving frequency, accuracy, and precision into your yoga journey, you're not just moving your body—you're cultivating awareness, discipline, and self-love. Show up, tune in, and move with intention. That's where the magic happens.
